Fetal Development Timeline

Week 4: Poppy Seed

Embryo is about 0.078 inches (2 mm) long

Weight: Less than 0.04 oz (1 gram)

Key Developments:

  • Blastocyst implants in uterine wall
  • Placenta begins to form
  • Amniotic sac develops
  • Primitive circulatory system starts

Health Tip

Start taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid to prevent neural tube defects.

Week 8: Raspberry

Baby is about 0.63 inches (16 mm) long

Weight: 0.04 oz (1 gram)

Key Developments:

  • Fingers and toes begin forming
  • Eyelids and ears developing
  • Heartbeat can be detected
  • All major organs have begun to form

Health Tip

Stay hydrated and eat small, frequent meals to combat morning sickness.

Week 12: Plum

Baby is about 2.1 inches (5.4 cm) long

Weight: 0.5 oz (14 grams)

Key Developments:

  • Reflexes begin developing
  • Fingers and toes fully separated
  • Eyelids formed but remain closed
  • Kidneys begin producing urine

Health Tip

Increase your intake of omega-3 fatty acids (found in salmon, walnuts, and flaxseeds) to support baby's brain development.

Week 16: Avocado

Baby is about 4.6 inches (11.6 cm) long

Weight: 3.5 oz (100 grams)

Key Developments:

  • Pattern of scalp hair beginning
  • Ears nearly in final position
  • Muscles getting stronger
  • Possible first flutters of movement

Health Tip

Start doing pelvic floor exercises (Kegels) to prepare for childbirth and prevent incontinence.

Week 20: Banana

Baby is about 6.5 inches (16.4 cm) long

Weight: 10.6 oz (300 grams)

Key Developments:

  • Vernix caseosa (protective coating) forms
  • Regular sleep/wake cycles established
  • Possible gender reveal at anatomy scan
  • Stronger, more noticeable movements

Health Tip

Sleep on your side (preferably left) to improve circulation to your baby. Use pillows for support.

Week 24: Corn

Baby is about 8 inches (20 cm) long

Weight: 1.3 lbs (600 grams)

Key Developments:

  • Lungs developing rapidly
  • Taste buds forming
  • Brain growing quickly
  • Skin becoming less transparent

Health Tip

Watch for signs of preterm labor like regular contractions, pelvic pressure, or fluid leakage.

Week 28: Eggplant

Baby is about 10 inches (25 cm) long

Weight: 2.2 lbs (1 kg)

Key Developments:

  • Eyes can open and close
  • Brain developing billions of neurons
  • Baby can dream during REM sleep
  • Lungs capable of breathing air

Health Tip

Get your Tdap vaccine to protect your baby from whooping cough after birth.

Week 32: Jicama

Baby is about 11.4 inches (29 cm) long

Weight: 3.75 lbs (1.7 kg)

Key Developments:

  • Practicing breathing movements
  • Bones fully formed but still soft
  • Skin becoming less wrinkled
  • Sleeping 90-95% of the day

Health Tip

Monitor baby's movements daily. Report any decrease in activity to your healthcare provider.

Week 36: Romaine Lettuce

Baby is about 12.5 inches (32 cm) long

Weight: 5.75 lbs (2.6 kg)

Key Developments:

  • Vernix covering begins to disappear
  • Baby may settle into head-down position
  • Lungs nearly fully developed
  • Skin becoming pink and smooth

Health Tip

Pack your hospital bag and finalize your birth plan.

Week 40: Pumpkin

Baby is about 14 inches (36 cm) long

Weight: 7.5 lbs (3.4 kg)

Key Developments:

  • Full-term development complete
  • Lanugo (fine hair) mostly gone
  • Nails may extend past fingertips
  • Ready for life outside the womb

Health Tip

Know the signs of labor: regular contractions, water breaking, or bloody show. Contact your provider when labor begins.
